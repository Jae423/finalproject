Gould Memorial Library (GML)

The Need for
a New Use

Situated on the CUNY Bronx Community College (BCC) campus, Gould Memorial Library is a stunning nationally landmarked building that fell into disrepair during the disinvestment of the Bronx in 1970’s. GML, modeled after the Pantheon in Rome, was constructed by the renowned architect Stanford White of McKim, Mead and White and the facility is recognized as among the supreme examples of White’s work. While the original purpose of the facility was to serve as a library, GML’s collection outgrew the building’s physical space.

Now, with the primary function no longer present in the facility, the main spaces remain largely vacant. The building is in need of restoration that is estimated to cost of $90 million. The future use of the edifice needs to be a compelling one for both the students and for the community. This website explores the context of the rehabilitation in order to inform adaptive reuse strategies and serve as a platform for information about the building’s adaptive reuse.

History of GML

The Building and BCC

BCC is a public college within the CUNY system. Its mission is to serve students of diverse backgrounds, preparations, and aspirations by providing them with an education that is both broad in scope and rigorous in its standards. Today, BCC enrolls 10,900 students in over 40 academic programs, and employs 1,800 faculty and staff. Over three-quarters (77%) of BCC students are Bronx residents. Recognized by the federal government as a Hispanic-Serving Institution, 61% of BCC students identify as Latino/Hispanic, while another 33% identify as Black, Non-Hispanic. Students represent over 100 countries and speak over 60 languages. A majority (55%) are the first in their families to attend college.

In 1892, New York University moved its undergraduate college from the densely-populated Washington Square campus to a then-rural location in the western part of the Bronx as the school  purchased the 45-acre site from the Mali Estate. NYU commissioned architect Stanford White, of the famed firm McKim, Mead, and White, to design their new campus. The complex of buildings is considered one of the finest concentrations of Beaux Arts architecture in the country. GML was, and remains, the architectural centerpiece of the campus. NYU operated its University Heights campus until the 1970s when, facing severe financial difficulties, it sold the property to CUNY for over $60 million. The timeline below describes the development of BCC and its subsequent move to the University Heights campus.

GML Timeline

1892

NYU purchases the 40 acre Mali estate to relocate its undergraduate campus to the more rural Bronx. In the same year, NYU commissions Stanford White to design the master plan for the campus.

1900

GML construction is completed and GML serves as the centerpiece of NYU's north campus from 1900-1972.

1950s

GML is significantly outmoded for its use as a library and NYU develops supplementary library facilities on campus. However, GML remains a library with ancillary classroom space until NYU relocates back to the Greenwich Village in the 1970s.

1966

The Stanford White Complex facilities are designated local landmarks by the New York City Landmarks Preservation Commission

1968

The GML book collection outgrows the space of the building and is moved to the new Engineering Library on campus.

1969

GML suffers fire damage in the basement auditorium from a molotov cocktail thrown by a protesting individual.

1973

NYU sells the campus to New York State Dormitory Authority (DASNY) for over $60 million. Bronx Community College (BCC) moves onto the former NYU University Heights campus later that year.

1978-1981

The Stanford White Complex facilities are listed on the New York State and National Registers of Historic Places. The interior of GML is designated a local landmark by the New York City Landmarks Preservation Commission.

2012

The Stanford White Complex is designated a National Historic Landmark in 2012, making BCC them the only community college in the country with that designation.

2016

Due it its state of disrepair, GML is named to the Preservation League of New York State’s 2016-17 Seven to Save Endangered Properties List.

Strategies for Reuse

Processes and Research

To discover an optimal use for the edifice, BCC enlisted a team of students at NYU Wagner School of Public Service to research potential uses. The team assessed existing conditions; researched repurposing efforts of other institutions with NHL designated facilities; developed a stakeholder map composed of students, government, businesses, and nonprofits; designed and executed a student survey; facilitated a stakeholder symposium; and interviewed other stakeholders.

The team presented a final report that incorporated conclusions based on their research, analysis, summary of the stakeholder symposium and engagement, and proposed reuse recommendations for GML.Below are the potential reuses of the building.
